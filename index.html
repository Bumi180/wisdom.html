Traditional Chinese Medicine

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) works by restoring balance in the body, focusing on overall wellness rather than just treating symptoms. At its core, TCM believes good health depends on the balance of Qi (vital energy), Yin-Yang, and the Five Elements.

  • Hot vs Cold Energy: If your body has too much yin or yang, it can drain your qi and create health problems.
  • Prevention Over Cure: Chinese medicine is restorative and promotes balance to break cycles of stress.
  • Common TCM Tea Principles: Ginger for warming, Green Tea for cooling, Chrysanthemum for clearing heat.

Wabi-Sabi

Wabi-sabi is a Japanese philosophy centered on the acceptance of transience and appreciation of beauty in imperfection. Developed out of the aesthetic philosophy of cha-no-yu (the tea ceremony) in fifteenth-century Japan.

Your tea doesn't need to be perfect. The chipped cup, the slightly burnt kettle, the herbs that didn't measure exactly right - these "imperfections" add character and authenticity to your practice.

The ceremony is an elegant and mindful practice that cherishes simplicity, naturalness, and the beauty of imperfection.

Ayurveda

Ayurveda is India's traditional, natural system of medicine that has been practiced for more than 5,000 years. A Sanskrit word, Ayurveda translates as "science of life" or "practices of longevity."

Vata

Air + Space
Qualities: Dry, cold, light, mobile

Tea needs: Warming spices like cinnamon and ginger

Pitta

Fire + Water
Qualities: Hot, oily, sharp, intense

Tea needs: Cooling herbs like peppermint and licorice

Kapha

Earth + Water
Qualities: Heavy, slow, cool, oily

Tea needs: Energizing herbs like anise and bay leaf
